SUMMARY OF AGENDA ITEM:
The agenda item involves a proposed project at 956 â€“ 966 South Vermont Avenue, which includes the demolition of two existing two-story commercial buildings to construct a new six-story mixed-use building. The new building will be approximately 89 feet high and will contain 90 residential units, with nine units reserved for Extremely Low Income households. Additionally, the project will include approximately 2,915 square feet of commercial space on the ground floor and provide 85 vehicle parking spaces across two subterranean levels and part of the ground floor. The project is subject to a partial appeal regarding the Director of Planning's determination of CEQA exemption and approval of density increase and incentives under the Transit Oriented Communities (TOC) Affordable Housing Incentive Program.

SUMMARY OF CPC DELIBERATIONS:
During the CPC meeting, the commission reviewed the proposed project, the appeal, and the Director of Planning's previous determinations. The deliberations included input from various representatives, including the applicant's representatives, the appellant, and a representative from Council District 10. The commission considered the project's compliance with CEQA guidelines, the TOC Affordable Housing Incentive Program, and the conditions of approval.

SUMMARY OF CPC MOTION:
Commissioner Perlman moved to approve the project as proposed, determining that the project is exempt from CEQA, denying the appeal, and sustaining the Planning Director's determination. The motion included approval of a 65 percent density increase and two incentives under the TOC program, approval of a Site Plan Review for the development, and adoption of the Conditions of Approval and Findings.

ALIGNMENT OF MOTION TO PROPOSAL:
The CPC voted to approve the agenda item, thereby denying the appeal and sustaining the Planning Director's determination.
