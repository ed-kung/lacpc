SUMMARY OF AGENDA ITEM:
The agenda item concerns the proposed development of a four-story, 45-foot tall residential building at 736 â€“ 738 East Vernon Avenue in Southeast Los Angeles. The project includes 48 dwelling units, with 13 reserved for Very Low Income households. It will provide 54 vehicular parking spaces and 44 bicycle parking spaces. The site currently has a single-family dwelling and two non-protected significant trees, which will be removed. The project requires grading of less than 500 cubic yards of soil. The requested actions include a CEQA exemption, a density bonus with an off-menu incentive for a front yard reduction, a conditional use permit for increased density, and a redevelopment plan project compliance review.

SUMMARY OF CPC DELIBERATIONS:
The CPC held a public hearing via Zoom webinar/teleconference, attended by city planning staff and the applicant's representative. Commissioner Hornstock moved to approve the project with the requested actions, and Commissioner Campbell seconded the motion. The commission discussed and voted on the motion.

SUMMARY OF CPC MOTION:
The motion proposed by Commissioner Hornstock included determining the project is exempt from CEQA, approving the density bonus with the off-menu incentive for a front yard reduction, approving the conditional use permit for increased density, approving the redevelopment plan project compliance review, adopting the conditions of approval, and adopting the findings.

ALIGNMENT OF MOTION TO PROPOSAL:
The CPC voted to approve the agenda item as proposed.
