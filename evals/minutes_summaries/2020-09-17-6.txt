ITEM NO. 6
DIR-2019-7067-TOC-1A

RELATED CASES:
DIR-2019-7067-TOC-1A, ENV-2019-5389-CE

SUMMARY OF AGENDA ITEM:
The agenda item concerns a proposed project at 5806 – 5812 West Lexington Avenue in Hollywood, Council District 13. The project involves demolishing two existing single-family structures and constructing a five-story, 56-foot tall, 17-unit multi-family dwelling. The building will have four residential levels over one at-grade parking level, providing 25 automobile parking spaces and bicycle parking. The project is part of the Transit Oriented Communities (TOC) Affordable Housing Incentive Program, with two units reserved for Extremely Low Income (ELI) households. An appeal was filed against the Planning Director’s Determination, which approved the project and granted certain incentives.

SUMMARY OF CPC DELIBERATIONS:
The City Planning Commission held a public hearing via Zoom webinar/teleconference. The deliberations included presentations from the City Planning staff, the applicant's representative, and the appellant's representative. The discussion focused on the project's compliance with CEQA guidelines and the TOC Affordable Housing Incentive Program, as well as the appeal against the Planning Director’s Determination.

SUMMARY OF CPC MOTION:
Commissioner Perlman proposed a motion to determine that the project is exempt from CEQA, deny the appeal, sustain the Planning Director’s Determination, approve the TOC Affordable Housing Incentive Program with additional incentives, and adopt the Conditions of Approval and Findings. Commissioner Khorsand seconded the motion.

ALIGNMENT OF MOTION TO PROPOSAL:
The CPC voted to approve the agenda item, denying the appeal and sustaining the Planning Director’s Determination.

VOTE TOTAL:
7-0

RESULT:
MOTION PASSED
