ITEM NO. 12
DIR-2021-7344-SPR-TOC-HCA-1A

RELATED CASES:
DIR-2021-7344-SPR-TOC-HCA-1A, ENV-2020-5078-CE

SUMMARY OF AGENDA ITEM:
The agenda item concerns the construction, use, and maintenance of a seven-story, mixed-use building at 550 South Union Avenue and adjacent addresses. The project includes approximately 105,620 square feet of floor area, with 13,046 square feet for commercial use and 88,398 square feet for residential use, resulting in a Floor Area Ratio (FAR) of 3.63:1. The project will replace two one-story commercial buildings and a parking lot, providing 100 dwelling units, 10 of which are reserved for Extremely Low Income Households. The building will have a maximum height of 92 feet and include 72 vehicle parking spaces, 125 long-term bicycle parking spaces, and 32 short-term bicycle parking spaces. The project also features 16,478 square feet of open space and requires the export of 21,400 cubic yards of earth and the removal of one non-protected tree.

SUMMARY OF CPC DELIBERATIONS:
The CPC held a public hearing with attendance from various stakeholders, including planning department representatives, appellants, and the applicant's representative. The meeting experienced a brief recess due to technical difficulties with translation headsets. The deliberations included reviewing the project's compliance with CEQA exemptions and the Transit Oriented Communities (TOC) Affordable Housing Incentive Program, as well as addressing appeals against the Planning Director’s determination.

SUMMARY OF CPC MOTION:
Commission President Millman moved to approve the project with modifications and amendments, determining it exempt from CEQA, denying the appeals, and sustaining the Planning Director’s determination. The motion included approving the TOC Affordable Housing Incentive Program with specific base and additional incentives, adopting modified conditions of approval, and adopting amended findings.

ALIGNMENT OF MOTION TO PROPOSAL:
The CPC voted to approve the agenda item with modifications and amendments.

VOTE TOTAL:
6-0

RESULT:
MOTION PASSED
