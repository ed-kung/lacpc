ITEM NO. 12
CPC-2019-1881-CA

RELATED CASES:  
CPC-2019-1881-CA, ENV-2019-1882-SE

SUMMARY OF AGENDA ITEM:  
The agenda item involves a proposed ordinance to amend Sections 12.80 and 12.81 of the Los Angeles Municipal Code (LAMC). The amendments are technical in nature and aim to align the city's regulations on emergency shelters with state law, specifically Government Code Section 8698 et seq. The City Planning Commission is required to hold a public hearing and consider several actions, including determining the ordinance's exemption from the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), recommending the ordinance's adoption by the City Council, and adopting the staff report and findings.

SUMMARY OF CPC DELIBERATIONS:  
During the meeting, the City Planning Commission held a public hearing on the proposed ordinance. Cally Hardy, a City Planning Associate, and Matthew Glesne, a City Planner, were present to represent the Department. The discussion focused on the technical amendments to the LAMC to ensure compliance with state emergency shelter regulations.

SUMMARY OF CPC MOTION:  
Commissioner Ambroz proposed a motion to approve the ordinance and recommend its adoption by the City Council. The motion included determining the ordinance's exemption from CEQA, adopting the staff report as the Commission's report, and adopting the findings. Commissioner Perlman seconded the motion.

ALIGNMENT OF MOTION TO PROPOSAL:  
The CPC voted to approve the agenda item, recommending the adoption of the proposed ordinance.

VOTE TOTAL:  
5-0

RESULT:  
MOTION PASSED
