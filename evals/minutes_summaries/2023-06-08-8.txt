ITEM NO. 8
DIR-2022-7636-TOC-SPR-VHCA-1A

RELATED CASES:
DIR-2022-7636-TOC-SPR-VHCA-1A, ENV-2022-7637-CE

SUMMARY OF AGENDA ITEM:
The agenda item concerns the construction, use, and maintenance of a new seven-story, approximately 108,000 square-foot mixed-use building located at 728 – 742 ½ South Western Avenue in the Wilshire Plan Area. The project includes 125 dwelling units, with 13 units set aside for Extremely Low Income Households, and a 3,920 square-foot commercial tenant space. The building will feature one level of subterranean parking, ground level parking, six residential levels, and a rooftop recreation space. The project also includes 10,350 square feet of open space for residents. An appeal was filed against the Director of Planning's determination that the project is exempt from CEQA and the approval of density increases and additional incentives under the Transit Oriented Communities Affordable Housing Incentive Program.

SUMMARY OF CPC DELIBERATIONS:
The City Planning Commission meeting on June 8, 2023, included attendance by representatives from the Department of City Planning, the applicant, and Council District 10. The deliberations focused on the proposed project, its compliance with CEQA exemptions, and the appeal filed by the Supporters Alliance for Environmental Responsibility (SAFER).

SUMMARY OF CPC MOTION:
Commissioner Lawshe moved to approve the project as proposed, determining that it is exempt from CEQA, denying the appeal, and sustaining the Planning Director’s Determination. The motion included approval of a 70 percent increase in density and additional incentives for reduced yard requirements and open space, as well as a Site Plan Review for the development. The motion also included adopting the Conditions of Approval and Findings.

ALIGNMENT OF MOTION TO PROPOSAL:
The CPC voted to approve the agenda item.

VOTE TOTAL:
6-0

RESULT:
MOTION PASSED
