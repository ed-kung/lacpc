                                       ITEM NO. 1                                   
                                                                                    
           DIRECTOR’S REPORT AND COMMISSION BUSINESS                                
                                                                                    
              • Vince P. Bertoni, AICP, Planning Director, announced effective January 7, 2019 the
                Department of City Planning will have three new Deputy Director’s, which are Shana M.
                Bonstin, Tricia Keane and Arthi L. Varma, AICP.                     
                                                                                    
                Arthi L. Varma, AICP, Principal Planner, gave an update on the status of the Home
                Sharing ordinance. On December 11, 2018, City Council adopted the Ordinance and
                there were several amendments.                                      
                                                                                    
              • Legal actions and issues update: Deputy City Attorney, Amy Brothers gave a legal
                update on Ponce vs City of Los Angeles stating there was a trial court decision involving
                a project that came before the City Planning Commission. Deputy City Attorney, Amy
                Brothers, gave a second legal update on Beverly Homes Association vs City of Los
                Angeles. The Appellate Court Decision upheld the trial court decision that was in the
                City’s favor.                                                       
              • Advanced Calendar: There were no changes to the advanced calendar.  
                                                                                    
              • Commission Requests: There were no requests.

           Commissioner Ambroz put forth the actions below in conjunction with the approval of the
           following project, with modifications, if any, stated on the record:     
                                                                                    
           A Vesting Tentative Tract Map on a 4.9-acre site for street vacation purposes.
                                                                                    
           1.   Find the that City Planning Commission has reviewed and considered the information
                contained in the Environmental Impact Report prepared for this project, which includes
                the Draft EIR, No. ENV-2012-2055-EIR (SCH No. 2014061066) dated March 2018, and
                the Final EIR, dated August 2018, and Erratum dated November 2018 (collectively
                known as the College Station Project EIR), as well as the whole of the administrative
                record.                                                             
                                                                                    
                Certify the following:                                              
                a.   The College Station Project EIR has been completed in compliance with the
                     California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA);                   
                b.   The College Station Project EIR was presented to the City Planning Commission
                     as a decision-making body of the lead agency; and              
                c.   The College Station Project EIR reflects the independent judgment and analysis
                     of the lead agency.                                            
                                                                                    
                Adopt all of the following:                                         
                a.  The related and prepared College Station Project Environmental Findings;
                b.  The Statement of Overriding;                                    
                c.  The Mitigation Monitoring Program prepared for the College Station Project EIR
                    Exhibit B in the Staff Recommendation Report.                   
           2.   Deny the appeal and sustain the Advisory Agency's determination to approve the
                Vesting Tentative Tract Map for the Project; and                    
           3.   Adopt the Conditions of Approval and Findings of the Director of Planning.

           MOTION:                                                                  
           Commissioner Ambroz put forth the actions below in conjunction with the approval of the
           following project, with modifications, if any, stated on the record:     
                                                                                    
           The construction, use and maintenance of a seven-story mixed-use development with up to 725
           multi-family residential units (37 units or five percent reserved for Very Low Income Households)
           and 51,600 square feet of commercial uses, totaling up to 618,580 square feet of floor area on
           a 4.92-acre vacant site. Residential uses would be located within five, five-story buildings with
           a maximum building height of 80 feet, arranged around a series of central courtyards on top of
           a two-story podium. The podium would contain parking uses wrapped with ground-floor
           commercial uses along College Street and Spring Street (including a 37,600 square-foot
           grocery market, 8,000 square feet of restaurants, and 6,000 square feet of retail uses). The
           Project includes the removal and export of approximately 80,000 cubic yards of soil for one level
           of subterranean parking.
